Apiculture in S. Sudan

South Sudan is a landlocked country; a member of the regional East Africa Community block and it’s the newest country in the world. The country has tropical climate and is rich in biodiversity including vast savanna, swamp land, very fertile soils and rain forests with the Nile River running through the country to the Mediterranean Sea. Utilizing these forests, the fertile soils and other resource in a sustainable way offers a good potential for socioeconomic development of this country that have suffered effects of many years of civil war. Beekeeping has a very long history in South Sudan and it was largely carried out as a social or a leisure activity where community members would go into the forest in search for the nice tasting wild honey from tree trunks or caves. However recently due to diseases and changes in life style, there has been a surge in the demand for honey in the world and production levels have not been able to match the currently demand. This in itself provides an opportunity for beekeepers in South Sudan to tap into the already existing market in the region as well as in the world market. Hagana is therefore pioneering to develop the country’s honey sector. We want to help the youth and women of South Sudan unlock their potential by asking them to partner with us and become our main producers and suppliers of honey and other agricultural produce that will feed our factory in the country’s capital Juba.

Havesting Bee Hive

Hagana works with farmers by using a geographical cluster system, where Honey is collected in containers that the company provides to farmers. This is to ensure that the quality of the honey remains unaltered.


Hagana Education

Hagana provides free training to farmers on all aspects of apiculture and apiary management, provides free buckets to farmers for easy of storage and harvest of honey as well as providing productive gears such as bee suits, smokers, bee brush, gumboot among other to maximize quality and friendliness to bees.
