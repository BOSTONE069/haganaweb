Introduction

Beekeeping has the potential to change lives of many young people and women in South Sudan as the sweetener has a very attractive price tag. It’s no longer carried out as a social or a leisure activity as was the case in the past where community members would go into the forest in search for wild honey from tree trunks or caves but as an economic activity carried out in modern apiaries or bee farms. With plenty of rain forests, South Sudan has the capacity to become a leading honey producing country in the region. Hagana, a South Sudanese youth-owned company, provides an essential link for honey producing farmers to tap into the vast local and international markets for honey. We strive to become a leading honey and honey- based products supplier in the country as well as internationally.

Core Values:

  • Quality: Adhere to product selection criteria that proudly provide a wide variety of organic and natural foods and products.
  • Ethical Business Practices: Maintain the highest ethical standards in our dealings with employees, customers and suppliers.
  • Teamwork: This is the collective responsibility we require from our team to offer optimal solutions with a focus of achieving common goals
  • Integrity: These are the fundamental values that make us different from others both in words and action

The primary objectives of Hagana’s initiative is to encourage local communities to engage in farming, contribute towards food security and raise rural incomes; create jobs along the agriculture value chains, enable people across South Sudan to access affordable and nutritious snacks; and diversify the local economy away from oil as we would export any surplus locally grown produce to regional markets..
